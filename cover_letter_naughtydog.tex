Dear Naughty Dog Hiring Team,

I'm applying for the Web Tools Programmer position. I want to be honest, this isn't just another application for me. Naughty Dog is the reason I got into this field in the first place.

I grew up in Turkey, and when I first played Uncharted with the Turkish dub, something clicked. Hearing Nathan Drake speak my language, seeing a game of that caliber acknowledge that Turkish players existed, that was priceless to me. For a kid surrounded by English-only media, it meant the world. That moment is what made me want to build things that could reach people everywhere.

I've spent the last few years as a full-stack engineer and the work lines up well with what you need. Right now I build and maintain internal web applications on AWS, handle database migrations at scale, and chase down distributed system issues across services. It's the same kind of tooling infrastructure that keeps a studio running day to day. Before that, I was at Oregon State for over two years where I broke apart a monolith into microservices, built front-end tools with Vue.js and TypeScript for internal teams, and wrote the documentation to keep it all maintainable.

Outside of work, I build games in C++ with SDL3 and I've been going through graphics programming with OpenGL. I'm also a Unity Certified Game Programmer. I got that certification because I wanted to understand game development from the engine level up. I won't pretend to be a senior engine programmer, but I genuinely enjoy the low-level work and I keep pushing myself in that direction.

What pulls me toward this role is the total project ownership. Integrating with the game runtime, architecting the backend, shipping functional UIs for the dev team. That's how I like to work. I care about making other developers' lives easier, and I would love to do that for the people building the games I grew up playing.

Thank you for your time. I would love the chance to talk more about how I can contribute.

Best, \\
